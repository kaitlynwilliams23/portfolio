Why Sports Analytics?

I have always had a passion for math, but I did not discover computer science until my sophomore year at Brown, when I took Andy van Dam's Object Oriented Coding class as a requirement for my Applied Math degree. After that, I fell in love with the challenges of coding, and learned that I could combine my passion for math with computer science, leading me to Data Science.

I have been fortunate enough to play varsity soccer at Brown, and I have witnessed first-hand how the collection and analysis of data is truly revolutionizing the world of sports. From deciding a playerâ€™s value and effect on a team to analyzing the statistical causes of injury among the athletes at Brown in an effort to minimize or otherwise avoid them, if possible, were some big discussion points among me, my coaching staff, and the athletics data science analyst.

This became all the more real to me after I tore my ACL during my senior season in Fall 2023. I have spent an extended amount of time in the training room rehabilitating my knee, learning a significant amount about the difference in injury rates of ACL tears between men and women, what factors can predict the timing and cause of an injury, and what steps could be taken to potentially prevent the injury altogether. The growing field of data science and how it is being used to change sports, both in how it is played and how we view it, is very exciting to me.
