Partiful Homepage Redesign

Project Overview

As a team, me, Brandon Wu, Will Park, and Kenneth Liou went through the iterative design process to redesign the homepage of our client, Partiful. We went through the process of designing a prototype from start to finish, including critique and iteration based on feedback

Timeline: April 2024

Background

Partiful is a new events platform and party RSVP website. It offers tools to create, customize, and share event invitations, allowing hosts to manage RSVPS, track guest lists, and communicate with attendees without having to keep someone's number. With a user-friendly interface, Partiful aims to make organizing parties and events more straightforward and enjoyable.

We were very excited to work with Partiful considering that every member of our group has used the interface to invite people to events. Having some experience with this program allowed us to explore some creative ideas we had to improve one of our favorite party invitation platforms.

Process

Our iterative design process for this client can be outlined by the following steps:

  • Reading the brief from the client
  • Asking clarifying questions to identify the core problems
  • Individually creating sketches of low-fidelity wireframes
  • Collaborating to settle on a design for our first round of low-fidelity wireframes
  • Receiving feedback from our peers
  • Sending the wireframes to our client, Partiful, to receive their feedback
  • Redesigning our low-fidelity wireframes as an intermediate step before creating the high-fidelity wireframes
  • Creating the high-fidelity interactive wireframe based off of the feedback we received from our client
  • Meeting with our client, Partiful, to show our final high-fidelity interactive wireframe and receiving feedback

Problem Definition

The clients at Partiful tasked us with redesigning their homepage for a more intuitive design to attract more users and improve navigation.

Some problems that they had expressed included the following:

  • Inability to see more than one or two events on the homepage at a time (limited surface area of h-scroll)
  • The difficulty of using h-scroll for every element on their homepage
  • The lack of content on the homepage other than events to bring users to the app even when they do not have any upcoming events
  • Keeping elements playful

Sketches

Once we established the main painpoints of Partiful's homepage in the eyes of its designers, we individually created sketches of the low-fidelity wireframe for the homepage, as seen below. Use the arrows at the bottom of the Figma display to scroll through some of our different sketches.

I got some of my main inspiration from Netflix in its categorial h-scroll with the ability to vertical scroll through different categories. Additionally, we thought of adding some kind of social aspect, similar to stories on Instagram or Snapchat, where users can post pictures from events.

First Iteration Wireframe

Based on the initial sketches, we created a first wireframe iteration. Shown below is our first Lo-Fi iteration. You can click on the elements on the screen to see some movement.

After sending a Loom video walking through our initial design, the feedback we received from our client was:

  • Make the information heirarchy much more clear
  • Make the events page the main page on the homepage
  • They loved the gumball idea, thinking that it was very fun
  • They wanted to hear more about what is on the "Trends" page and how that content was generated (through users or companies or both)
  • They challenged us to think more outside the box in displaying events and to think of something that displays more events at a time than a card carousel

Second Iteration Wireframe

Based on the feedback, we refined our wireframes for the second iteration. You can interact with the Figma file by interacting with elements on the app and clicking the arrows at the bottom to switch between tabs.

Highlights:

  • The events page is the starting page
  • Moved the navigation bar to the top of the screen
  • We chose to go with an Apple Wallet style of design to display invites and upcoming events, in order to display multiple events on a single page
  • Kept the Netflix style of scrolling on both the "Trends" and "Friends" pages

Hi-Fi Prototyping/Final Product

We created high-fidelity prototypes for final review. Below is the embedded Figma design. You can interact with the prototype by vertical scrolling, swiping on the different events, clicking through the navigation tabs, and clicking highlights in the "Friends" tab. Additionally, you can see what different homepages would look like depending on the number of invites and upcoming events a user has.

Highlights:

  • We implemented an Apple Wallet display of events, where the events are stacked on top of each other
  • For incoming events, the user can swipe left/right to show if they are attending or not
  • The user can scroll vertically to see upcoming events and past events
  • The user can click on "Trends" to navigate to the "Trends" page where they will find party themes, resturants/food, and venues
  • The user can click on the "Friends" page to navigate to users' highlights where a user can click on the highlight and see a polariod picture post from the party
  • The user can return to the "Friends" page to see their friends and suggested users

Client Feedback

After completing our Hi-Fi design, we sent a Loom video of our design and got to meet with the product designers of Partiful as well to show what we produced based on the feedback and multiple iterations that we thought of.

The feedback we received was very positive. They liked the design we created and thought it was a significant improvement over their current homepage. They also provided us with some suggestions for further improvement, which we will incorporate into our final design.

Here are some of the key points from the feedback we received:

  • They liked the Apple Wallet style of viewing the events
  • All of the points of feedback and criteria to hit were answered in the Hi-Fi
  • Interactions should be generalized where a swiping motion should signify only one of reorganization or stating "Going"
  • The Apple Wallet should also have an interaction to open up to see all the events at once instead of it being stacked which will help accesibility.
  • In the trends tab, the content should be geared more towards getting inspiration to help hosts.
  • In the friends tab, more of an activity page seeing people going to parties isntead of just seeing what people there are
  • They loved the polaroid picture UI in the friends tab as well as the highlights section.

All the feedback was really helpful in identifying which direction to take the design and help thorough out the next steps in making this a fully thought out design.

Conclusions

Key Takeaways:

  • The importance of iterative design and how it can help us create a better product
  • How to work with a client and incorporate their feedback into our designs

Some things we could consider if we continued to process in the future would be creating the "Trends" page to be more geared towards helping hosts plan events. This could look like creating some kind of Tinder-like swiping feature for users to swipe through and "like" themes, food options, and venues for events.

I want to give many thanks to the Partiful team for giving us the opportunity to work with them and for providing us with valuable feedback throughout the design process.
